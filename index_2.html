Further changes to the labelling of Non-prescription Drugs

Under the self-care framework [link to SCF landing page], Health Canada is looking to update its approach to regulating self-care products such as cosmetics, natural health products and non-prescription drugs. Phase II of the framework will introduce, for consultation, targeted amendments to the Food and Drug Regulations to introduce a risk-based approach to regulatory oversight for non-prescription drugs. These changes are intended to align the oversight for non-prescription drugs with other self-care products of comparable level of risk. For cosmetic-like products, a Drug Facts table will be voluntary. For information regarding the labelling of these low-risk, cosmetic-like products, see Labelling of Category I products [Link to Cat I labelling description].

Labelling of Category I products

Category I products include toothpastes, mouthwashes, secondary sunscreens, and acne washes as well as products for local use on unbroken skin such as: antiseptic skin cleansers, anti-dandruff shampoos, medicated skin-care products, and diaper rash creams.
